Meet Our Staff

Rev. Brian Jackson,

Ex-Officio

Rev. Brian Jackson serves as the proud pastor of James Island Church USA in Charleston, South Carolina, a congregation of 1000 members doing ministry in Charleston and beyond. Dr. Henderson has held several positions in the Presbyterian Church such as Moderator of the Presbytery of the Mid-South, chair of the presbytery’s Coordinating Council, delegate to the General Assembly, member of the Presbytery Commission on Ministry, and member of the Finance Committee for the Synod of the Living Waters. Dr. Henderson also serves as a trustee for Stillman College, a member of the Mayor’s Clergy Council in Charleston, South Carolina, and a member of the James Island Interfaith Council which is an ecumenical group of ministry leaders on James Island.

Dr. Henderson holds a bachelor degree in Finance from the University of Southern Mississippi, a Master’s degrees from Johnson C. Smith Seminary and Memphis Theological Seminary, and a Doctor of Ministry in Leadership from Memphis Theological Seminary.

Jackie Brown,

Chairperson

Jackie Brown serves as the Chairperson of The St. James Foundation. She has been serving in this capacity since 2016. She brings a wealth of knowledge to this Foundation after serving on the Board other non-profits since 2004.

Jackie retired from SPAWAR System Center Charleston in 2016 after 37 years of service as a Program Analyst. During her employment she became Green Belt certified in Process Improvements. She joined the Board of Directors of the St. James Foundation where she served as Secretary of the organization for 5+ years. In 2004 Jacqueline was one of the founding members of the Twenty Pearls Foundation, a charitable 501c3 non-profit organization. She served as secretary, vice president and president. One of the main purposes of this organization is to provide scholarships to high school graduates pursuing a higher education. Jacqueline also served as a trustee on the Embracing U Foundation which is a Breast cancer awareness organization whose main purpose is to educate, embrace, and empower breast cancer survivors.

Jackie received a BS degree from Spelman College in 1978. She believes education is our rights of passage in life. She also believes that serving on The St. James Foundation provides her an opportunity to help the underserved, the voiceless and to improve the conditions of James Island and the surrounding communities.

Charles Hayward,

Secretary

Charles Hayward faithfully served the St. James Island Church USA as Pastor for twenty years, May 1996 to April 2016. He is the organizing and founding Pastor of The St. James Foundation. He has served the Presbyterian Church USA in all councils of the denomination and is regarded for his community leadership role in the areas of community service, social justice, public education and entrepreneurship.

Davonni Gray,

Treasurer

Davonni Gray has worked in Charleston as a project researcher and a Clinical Health Research Coordinator for the past 18 years. During that time, she worked independently to implement projects from start to close and mentored other Research Coordinators. She earned a Master of Public Health degree from the University of South Carolina and then lived in Cameroon, Africa for 18 years before returning to Charleston.

Deborah is a small business owner and currently works as a real estate investor. She has a strong desire to help others which has been evident in her profession and in volunteer work in the community. She volunteers for several community organizations; the neighborhood civic association as secretary, the Charleston Area Justice Ministry as Team Leader and Housing Steering Committee Member and has served on personnel and event committees and ministries at the St James Presbyterian Church.
