Our Mentoring Program

Purpose: to assist students especially those at-risk with behavioral and academic challenges.

Five-Steps Program for “behavioral modification,” by:

  1. Acknowledging the error of their behavior.
  2. Accepting the disciple associated with the error.
  3. Apologizing to those who have been offended.
  4. Identifying Alternative behavior that would have been appropriate.
  5. Developing an Action Plan for correcting the error in behavior.

The program has mentored approximately 190 young men.

The main benefit: the mentoring relationships between the volunteer men and the young men.

An additional benefit has been the support that the mentors have forged for one another.

The support of the St. James Presbyterian Church and membership are invaluable
